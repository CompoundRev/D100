Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can I get an appointment?

Do you accept insurance for medical dermatology visits?

▼

Yes, we work with most major insurance plans for medical dermatology services including treatment of acne, eczema, psoriasis, rashes, and skin cancer screening. Our team will verify your coverage before your appointment.

What's the difference between medical and cosmetic dermatology?

▼

Medical dermatology treats skin diseases and conditions (acne, rosacea, eczema, skin cancer). Cosmetic dermatology focuses on aesthetic enhancement (wrinkle reduction, skin rejuvenation, volume restoration). We offer both because many patients need elements of each for complete skin health.

How do I know if my skin concern is serious enough to see a specialist?

▼

If you're worried about it, it's worth having checked. Concerning moles, persistent rashes, chronic acne, or any changes in your skin deserve evaluation by a dermatologist. Early diagnosis often leads to simpler, more effective treatment.

What if I've tried treatments before and nothing worked?

▼

Many patients come to us after unsuccessful treatments elsewhere. We take a comprehensive approach, often combining therapies in customized protocols, and we stay with you through adjustments until we find what works for your specific skin.

Are cosmetic treatments obvious or natural-looking?

▼

Our philosophy is conservative enhancement that looks natural. We're not interested in overdone results. The goal is for people to notice you look refreshed and great, not to be able to identify specific procedures.
