Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know rhinoplasty will actually improve my appearance and not make things worse?

▼
Dr. Holley uses Vectra 3D imaging during your consultation to show you a detailed visualization of your potential results before you ever commit to surgery. You'll see exactly how changes to your nose will affect your overall facial harmony, eliminating guesswork and building confidence in your decision.

Will people be able to tell I had a nose job?

▼
Dr. Holley specializes in natural-looking results that preserve your ethnic identity and unique facial character. The goal is refinement and balance - a nose that looks like it's always belonged on your face, not an obvious surgical result.

How long is the recovery, and will I be able to work?

▼
Most patients take 1-2 weeks off work. Swelling and bruising are most noticeable in the first week, with significant improvement by week two. Dr. Holley provides detailed recovery protocols so you can plan accordingly and know what to expect at every stage.

How much does rhinoplasty cost?

▼
Pricing varies based on your specific surgical plan and whether functional correction is included. West Michigan Plastic Surgery offers flexible financing options to make your investment more accessible. Exact pricing will be discussed during your consultation.

I'm worried about pain during recovery. How bad is it really?

▼
Most patients report that rhinoplasty recovery is easier than they expected. Discomfort is typically managed well with prescribed medication in the first few days, and many patients describe it as pressure rather than sharp pain.

How do I know Dr. Holley is the right surgeon for me?

▼
Dr. Holley has over 20 years of dedicated rhinoplasty experience and is board-certified. During your consultation, you'll see extensive before-and-after photos of patients with similar nose types, discuss his surgical approach, and get a clear sense of whether his philosophy aligns with your goals.

When will I see my final results?

▼
You'll see significant improvement within 2-4 weeks as swelling subsides. However, final results continue to refine over 12 months as residual swelling fully resolves. The results are permanent.
