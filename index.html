Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the All-on-4 procedure actually take?

▼

The implant surgery itself typically takes 2-3 hours per arch. You'll receive a temporary set of teeth the same day, allowing you to leave with a complete smile. Your permanent prosthetic is placed after initial healing, usually within a few months.

Will I have to go without teeth during treatment?

▼

Absolutely not. One of the biggest advantages of All-on-4 is that you receive a functional set of temporary teeth on the same day as your surgery. You'll never be without teeth.

I've been told I don't have enough bone for implants. Can All-on-4 still work?

▼

In most cases, yes. The angled placement of All-on-4 implants maximizes existing bone and often eliminates the need for bone grafting. A 3D scan during your consultation will determine if you're a candidate.

How painful is the recovery?

▼

Most patients report that recovery is far more comfortable than they expected. You'll receive detailed post-op care instructions and medication to manage any discomfort. Many patients return to work within a few days and are eating normally within weeks.

What can I eat after the procedure?

▼

You'll stick to soft foods for the first few weeks while your implants integrate with the bone. After full healing, you can eat virtually anything—steak, apples, corn on the cob—without restrictions.

What's the investment for All-on-4, and do you offer financing?

▼

Treatment investment varies based on your specific needs and chosen materials. We offer flexible financing options to make this life-changing procedure accessible. During your consultation, we'll provide a detailed cost breakdown and discuss payment plans that work for your budget.

How long do All-on-4 implants last?

▼

With proper care, All-on-4 implants can last 20+ years or even a lifetime. The implants themselves integrate permanently with your jawbone. The prosthetic teeth may need minor adjustments over time but are built for long-term durability.
