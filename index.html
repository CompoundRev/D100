Frequently Asked Questions

Will I look like I've had a facelift?

No. The goal of modern facelift surgery is to create natural, refreshed results - not a pulled or overdone appearance. Dr. Holley's approach focuses on restoring youthful contours while preserving your unique facial characteristics and expressions.

How long is the recovery, and when can I return to work?

Most patients return to work within 2-3 weeks wearing makeup to cover residual bruising. Initial swelling subsides within 10-14 days, though complete healing takes 3-6 months. Dr. Holley provides detailed recovery guidelines tailored to your lifestyle.

What's the difference between a traditional facelift and a deep plane facelift?

A deep plane facelift repositions deeper facial structures for more dramatic, longer-lasting results with a natural appearance. Dr. Holley will recommend the technique best suited to your anatomy and goals during your consultation.

Am I too old (or too young) for a facelift?

Age is less important than your specific concerns and overall health. Patients in their 40s may benefit from a mini facelift, while those in their 60s and 70s often achieve excellent results with traditional techniques. Dr. Holley evaluates each patient individually.

How long do facelift results last?

A well-performed facelift typically provides 10-15 years of improvement. While aging continues, you'll maintain a more youthful baseline than if you hadn't had surgery. Many patients find this one-time investment far more cost-effective than ongoing non-surgical treatments.

Can I combine a facelift with other procedures?

Absolutely. Many patients combine facelifts with neck lifts, brow lifts, eyelid surgery, or fat grafting for comprehensive facial rejuvenation. Dr. Holley will design a customized treatment plan based on your unique needs and desired outcomes.
